# Nivvi Pre-Seed Investor Deck Content

## Summary

This document is the decision-complete content package for a 12-slide pre-seed deck.

Deck objective:
- Raise: `$750K-$1M` (USD)
- Audience: generalist pre-seed funds
- Stage narrative: prototype built, pre-launch
- Positioning lead: `Nivvi, your personal AI money manager.`

Rules:
- No fabricated traction, revenue, or customer metrics
- No claims beyond prototype/pre-launch truth
- Use `agentic` only when paired with `supervised`

## Slide 1 - Cover

Headline:
- `Nivvi, your personal AI money manager`

Subhead:
- `Advisor-level whole-money management for households`

Content bullets:
- `You approve. Nivvi handles execution.`

One-line takeaway:
- Nivvi competes with advisor-level financial management, not just finance tools.

Founder script (30-60s):
- Households already use many financial products, but no product manages the whole system end to end.
- Nivvi does: detect what matters, prepare the move, execute after approval.
- We are building the management layer households do not have today.

## Slide 2 - Problem

Headline:
- `Financial life is fragmented.`

Content bullets:
- Money is split across banks, cards, loans, brokers, taxes, and utilities.
- Decisions are interconnected, but tools are siloed.
- Advice and execution are separate, so users still do the work.
- Outcomes: missed deadlines, idle cash, avoidable fees, admin burden.

One-line takeaway:
- Households do not have a coordinated manager for their financial system.

Founder script (30-60s):
- Today, people chain together dashboards, budgeting apps, brokers, and tax tools.
- Each tool solves one slice, none coordinates tradeoffs across the whole system.
- The job to be done is management, not more visibility.

## Slide 3 - Category / Why Existing Tools Fail

Headline:
- `The real competition is advisors and DIY financial admin.`

Content bullets:
- Primary competitor: human advisor model (high cost, limited access).
- Default fallback: DIY management across spreadsheets and disconnected apps.
- Trackers, aggregators, and chatbots are substitutes but stop short of management.
- Nivvi = personal AI money manager that prepares and executes approved actions across the whole system.

One-line takeaway:
- Nivvi wins by delivering advisor-level management at software scale.

Founder script (30-60s):
- The user alternative is either expensive advisor support or doing everything alone.
- Existing finance tools provide slices of functionality but do not manage the whole system.
- Nivvi is the management layer: software-level affordability with cross-domain execution.

## Slide 4 - Solution

Headline:
- `Nivvi is your agentic, supervised AI money manager for the whole household financial system.`

Content bullets:
- Detect -> prioritize -> prepare action -> approve -> execute -> monitor/adapt.
- Connects to existing financial products users already have.
- Includes portfolio opportunity intelligence: mandate/risk/tax-aware proposal drafting for holding rotation and rebalancing.
- Executes only after explicit approval for consequential actions.

One-line takeaway:
- Agency plus supervision: action without loss of control.

Founder script (30-60s):
- Agentic is not a buzzword for us; it means the product carries work through.
- Supervised is equally non-negotiable; user approval gates consequential execution.
- This is the advisor loop turned into software.

## Deck QA Checklist

- Clarity test: an investor can describe Nivvi in one sentence within 5 seconds.
- Category test: investor can distinguish Nivvi from advisor services, DIY workflows, and finance tools.
- Credibility test: no fabricated metrics, no post-launch claims.
- Trust test: approval, reliability, and auditability are explicit.
- Fundability test: clear wedge, milestones, and use-of-funds logic.
- Consistency test: uses canonical language from `docs/positioning.md`.

## Final Defaults

- Narrative mode: prototype built, pre-launch.
- Geography: NL-first wedge with EU expansion path.
- Messaging: `agentic` is prominent and always paired with `supervised`.
- Evidence mode: product behavior proof, no traction inflation.
